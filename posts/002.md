# TMUX AND OTHER FUN STUFF

*2026 03 09*

One of the things that made my interest in spending more time in the terminal spike was when I found out about Tmux.

For thos poor souls out there that don't know - Tmux is a terminal multiplexer. For me, who is not very savvy with Tmux (yet), it's basically a way to have a bunch of terminal apps running in different tabs with layouts to your own liking.

Below is a screenshot of my most used setup. On my first tab I always have IRC (weechat), and on the second there is always a lynx web browser, musikcube, and cava.

[![](/assets/tmux01.png "my tmux setup")](/assets/tmux01.png)

"But why on earth would you ever need Tmux to do that? I can do that on my GNOME Terminal!" Yeah well first of all I do what I want. And second - if I SSH into a server or whatever, it is such a life saver being able to look things up in a lynx window, while changing something in VIM in the other. And it basically just works, flawlessly.

I do know however that there are a bunch of cool things about Tmux that I'm not talking about here, like restoring sessions, detaching, and a million other stuffs.

I don't think I have my dotfiles up on my github with my current Tmux config, but I'll look into it.

Keep it cool.
